Financial Conflict of Interest Policy for PHS Funded Research

1. Purpose

This policy ensures that government-funded research remains unbiased. It implements federal regulations at 42 CFR Part 50 and 45 CFR Part 94 governing financial conflicts of interest in PHS-funded research.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all PHS-funded research conducted by Dimagi, with the exception of Phase I SBIR/STTR applications.

3. Definitions

  • Clinical Trial: A research study involving human participants to evaluate the effect of one or more interventions.
  • Disclosure: A report of financial interests submitted by an Investigator.
  • Family: The Investigator's spouse or domestic partner and dependent children.
  • Financial Interest: Anything of monetary value, whether or not the value is readily ascertainable.
  • FCOI: A Financial Conflict of Interest, meaning a significant financial interest that could directly and significantly affect the design, conduct, or reporting of PHS-funded research.
  • Institutional Official: Krishna Swamy, Senior Director of Corporate Operations.
  • Investigator: The principal investigator and any other person responsible for the design, conduct, or reporting of research funded by PHS.
  • PHS: The Public Health Service, an operating division of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.
  • Research: A systematic investigation designed to develop or contribute to generalizable knowledge.
  • Significant Financial Interest: A financial interest of $5,000 or more in value, or ownership interest of any amount, in a publicly or non-publicly traded entity.

4. Policy Requirements

Investigators must submit annual disclosures of significant financial interests. Ad hoc disclosures must be submitted within 30 days of acquiring a new financial interest or becoming aware of an existing one relevant to PHS-funded research. Travel disclosures are required for reimbursed or sponsored travel. The Institutional Official will review all disclosures prior to the expenditure of PHS funds. Clinical trial disclosure requirements apply as specified by federal regulation. Dimagi will report identified financial conflicts of interest to the relevant PHS agency within 60 days of identification. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. All Investigators must complete FCOI training at least every four years. Records related to this policy will be retained for at least three years following the submission of the final expenditure report. Disclosures will be treated as confidential to the extent permitted by law. Information on identified FCOIs will be made publicly accessible within five business days of a request.
