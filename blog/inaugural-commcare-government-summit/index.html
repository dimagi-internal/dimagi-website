Key Takeaways from Dimagi’s Inaugural CommCare Government Summit

64 health leaders from 11 countries gathered in Addis Ababa to explore how to scale national digital health systems across Africa, sustainably.

SS
Sarah Strauss Dec 2024 4 min read Events
Share LinkedIn X

Delegates at the inaugural CommCare Government Summit, Addis Ababa, October 2024.

In October 2024, Dimagi hosted its inaugural CommCare Government Summit in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, bringing together 64 health leaders from 11 countries to explore how to scale digital health across Africa.

Inaugural CommCare Government Summit
64 leaders, 11 countries

Co-sponsored by The Global Fund, the summit centered on the building blocks of successful digital health programs: interoperability, governance, infrastructure, and workforce capacity.

Bringing diverse perspectives together

Attendees included Ministry of Health representatives from countries using CommCare at national scale, among them Benin, Burkina Faso, Ethiopia, Madagascar, Senegal, South Africa, Côte d’Ivoire, and Niger, alongside funders and international NGOs including USAID, the Gates Foundation, the World Health Organization, Living Goods, Last Mile Health, and Catholic Relief Services. More than half of attendees came from Francophone countries, with live translation throughout.

Opening remarks and keynote

Ethiopia’s State Minister of Health, Dr. Ayele Teshome, delivered the keynote, sharing insights from the country’s digital health transformation, particularly its eCHIS (electronic Community Health Information) system, which already serves over 25 million people, with plans to reach even more in the coming years.

Three days of collaboration

Over three days, delegates joined presentations and panels, live demos showing how countries use CommCare to strengthen their health workforce (including biometrics, support for low-literacy workers, and campaign integration), hands-on booths, and an emerging-tech tour exploring AI for equitable outcomes and how CommCare Connect lets frontline workers opt into additional paid work.

Looking forward

The message was clear: governments are committed to strengthening community health systems but face mounting pressure to do more with fewer resources. With funding under strain, sustainability is no longer optional, which is why we’ve drawn on 20 years of CommCare to launch a next-generation national eCHIS that’s 10x more affordable for governments.
