Empowering Public Health Workers in Somerville with Digital Tools

How Somerville’s public health nurses and community health workers are using CommCare to streamline workflows, improve data sharing, and deliver more coordinated care.

D
Dimagi Jul 2025 4 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Somerville is equipping its public health teams with CommCare.

The City of Somerville, Massachusetts has long shown a strong commitment to community health. Its Public Health Nurses and Community Health Workers support residents across a wide range of needs, from vaccine clinics and health screenings to disease tracking and connecting people to vital resources, but their work has been constrained by fragmented systems, duplicative data entry, and time-intensive manual processes. To address this, Somerville is partnering with Dimagi to implement CommCare, a secure, human-centered case management platform for community-based public health.

Building future-ready public health technology

With CommCare, Somerville is laying the groundwork for a more integrated, responsive public health system. The platform streamlines intake and documentation for nurses and community health workers while improving data accuracy, enhancing privacy compliance, and enabling real-time insights and reporting.

Features such as role-based data access, digital consent tracking, and sophisticated reporting strengthen collaboration while protecting client privacy. By establishing a shared digital system, the city is better equipped to identify service gaps, track referrals, and deliver more coordinated, person-centered care.

The investment also positions Somerville for what lies ahead. As policies evolve around Medicaid reimbursement and funding for community-based care, the ability to document services and demonstrate impact will be essential, and CommCare’s flexible architecture provides the tools to support it.

“We’re excited to provide Somerville’s community and public health workers with the tools they need to tackle pressing public health challenges. Their proactive approach will set their workers and community up for success down the line.”

Jonathan Jackson, CEO, Dimagi

From local investment to lasting impact

Somerville joins a growing movement of municipalities recognizing the value of digital infrastructure in community health, including Island Health Care on Martha’s Vineyard, which in 2023 implemented CommCare to improve coordination between community health workers, population health nurses, and local Boards of Health.

By embracing technology to eliminate inefficiencies and enhance service delivery, Somerville isn’t only responding to today’s challenges, it’s building the foundation for a more connected, equitable, and resilient system of care, where frontline public health workers are supported with the tools they need.
