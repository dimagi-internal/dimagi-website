Awards

A look at the awards, certifications, and working groups that reflect Dimagi’s commitment to open source, impact, and a collaborative work culture.

D
Dimagi Feb 2020 2 min read Company
Share LinkedIn X

Dimagi has been recognized for its commitment to open source software and impact-focused work culture.

Dimagi has been recognized for its uncompromising commitment to open source software and to an interactive, collaborative work culture that fosters partnerships across impact-focused organizations around the world. Our culture, work environment, and dedication to impact have earned awards for everything from our governance structure to our focus on our customers and partners.

Awards

Below are just a few of the awards we’ve received:

  • Bloomberg Business Week Most Promising Social Entrepreneurs
  • Schwab Foundation Social Entrepreneur of the Year
  • Fast Company World Changing Ideas Finalist
  • Inc Fastest Growing Companies Awards
  • Inc Magazine Best Places to Work
  • Built In Boston Best Midsize Companies to Work For
  • Path PHC Tech Challenge Award for Digital Health Innovations
  • Elasticsearch Cause Awards
  • B Corp “Best of the World” Award
  • Vodafone Foundation Mobile for Good Award
  • Mastercard Connected World Awards
  • GBCHealth Business Action in Health Awards
  • UCSF Health Awards 2021 Finalist for Breakthrough Covid-19 Technology
  • Legatum FORTUNE Technology Prize Finalist
  • USAID awards, including Development Innovation Ventures Stage 1 (2010) and Stage 2 (2013), WomenConnect (2019), Zika Grand Challenges (2016), Ebola Grand Challenges (2016), Saving Lives at Birth awards (various), and others.

Certifications

In addition to awards, below are a few of Dimagi’s certifications. Please note that this list is not exhaustive.

  • Certified as a B Corporation and incorporated as a Benefit Corporation in Massachusetts
  • Climate Neutral Certification
  • Various tech certifications, including that Dimagi’s CommCare solution is certified SOC 2 compliant (see more in our security overview)

Working groups

Dimagi and members of our team are representatives on several leading technical working groups, consortiums, and networks. These cut across a variety of relevant areas, including international development, social entrepreneurship, technology for good, and various public health areas such as malaria, maternal newborn and child health, and community health.

This is a partial list of these initiatives:

  • WHO SMART Vaccination Certificate Consortium
  • Digital Health & Interoperability Working Group (DH&IWG)
  • Schwab Foundation for Social Entrepreneurship
  • World Health Organization, Roster of Digital Health Experts
  • Principles for Digital Development
  • Community Health Impact Coalition
  • One Million Community Health Workers Campaign
  • Innovations in Healthcare Network
  • B Corp Network
  • Catalyst 2030
  • CHW Central
  • The Digital Square Global Goods Community
  • Business Call to Action
  • Linux Foundation Public Health
  • Digital Solutions for Malaria Elimination (DSME) Community of Practice
  • OpenMRS Advisory Council
  • OpenHIE Community of Practice
  • Coalition for Access to NCD Medicines and Products
  • UN Commission on Life Saving Commodities
