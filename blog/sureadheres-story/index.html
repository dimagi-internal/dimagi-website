SureAdhere’s Story

From a 2009 research project at UC San Diego to a globally recognized tool for tuberculosis treatment adherence: the story of SureAdhere, now part of Dimagi.

MK
Michael Kelleher Dec 2024 3 min read
Share LinkedIn X

SureAdhere brings video-supported treatment to tuberculosis programs worldwide.

SureAdhere’s journey began in 2009, when researchers at the University of California San Diego pioneered the world’s first video Directly Observed Therapy (VDOT) intervention for tuberculosis. Over six years, the team evaluated the approach before spinning the technology off into a social enterprise, and in 2022 SureAdhere joined Dimagi.

Our founding

Collaborating closely with tuberculosis programs in California and Baja California, Mexico, the UCSD team built and rigorously tested VDOT, a way for patients to record and submit videos of taking their medication rather than being observed in person. The mission was simple: make VDOT readily available to TB programs across the globe, even in regions with low connectivity.

The early days

Working with TB providers in resource-limited settings, we developed best practices to help National TB Programs implement VDOT. Those efforts led to a pivotal milestone in 2017, when the World Health Organization conditionally approved video-supported treatment as a replacement for in-person Directly Observed Therapy.

WHO milestone
2017

Video Directly Observed Therapy conditionally approved as a replacement for in-person DOT, with the growing evidence base later strengthening the WHO’s Video Supported Treatment guidelines.

To further validate the approach, the CDC compared video-observed therapy to in-person DOT using the SureAdhere platform. Combined with evidence accumulated since 2010, the results led the WHO to strengthen its guidelines, and in March 2023, on World TB Day, the CDC published an article endorsing VDOT as a comparable alternative to in-person DOT.

Joining forces with Dimagi

In March 2022, SureAdhere was acquired by Dimagi, a leader in high-impact digital health. The acquisition expanded Dimagi’s portfolio of virtual care platforms and accelerated progress toward a shared vision: a world where everyone has access to the essential services they need to thrive. As part of the Dimagi family, SureAdhere continues its mission of transforming treatment adherence and improving outcomes for patients worldwide.
