The Apps Dimagi Deployed for COVID-19 Response

From WHO-protocol contact tracing to vaccine delivery, screening, and rapid-test monitoring: the CommCare apps Dimagi deployed with governments and partners during the pandemic.

D
Dimagi Sep 2021 4 min read Global Health
Share LinkedIn X

The CommCare COVID-19 Response template app: register patients, trace contacts, track suspected cases, and manage lab results.

When COVID-19 spread across the world in 2020, Dimagi moved quickly to put digital tools in the hands of the governments, ministries, and partners leading the response. Built on CommCare, our open-source platform and a recognized digital Global Good, these applications could be deployed to Android devices in days and adapted to local contexts. Here is a look at the apps we deployed with partners during the pandemic.

Contact tracing built on the WHO protocol

Early in the pandemic, Dimagi released a free CommCare template application implementing the full World Health Organization FFX protocol for case reporting and contact tracing. Organizations could import it into a CommCareHQ project space, deploy it immediately to Android devices, and customize it to local needs. The app was made available in French, Portuguese, Spanish, and Hindi, alongside a public COVID-19 application library and a set of training resources.

Governments built on that foundation. In Sierra Leone, the Directorate of Science, Technology and Innovation (DSTI) and Dimagi formed a joint team to rapidly develop a contact tracing application tailored to COVID-19, drawing on lessons from the country’s Ebola response to decentralize tracing and strengthen data collection and public health messaging. The work brought together partners including MIT, Statistics Sierra Leone, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, the eGovernance Academy, UNICEF Sierra Leone, and UNDP.

“As we saw during the previous Ebola outbreak in West Africa, digital technology can play a critical role in improving the impact of the outbreak response. Through this partnership with DSTI, we will leverage leading best practices in developing critical technology to rapidly respond to and curtail the impact of COVID-19 in Sierra Leone.”

Jonathan Jackson, CEO, Dimagi

Screening and triage at the front line

With Terre des hommes and partners, in collaboration with Taiwan ICDF, Dimagi deployed COVID-19 apps in Jharkhand, India: a triage and screening application used by intake personnel for every patient arriving at primary health centers, paired with a conversational in-service agent for health workers. Encouraged by early results, the program doubled in scale from 80 to 160 healthcare workers, adding new workflows to help staff comply with the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare’s Infection Prevention and Control standards.

Building on the India deployment, Terre des hommes and Dimagi replicated the approach in Nepal, adapting existing template applications, screening and triage based on the WHO FFX protocol app and a healthcare-provider training and monitoring chatbot, for six target hospitals. The tools digitized paper-based screening workflows and delivered standardized training, reducing reliance on ad hoc channels like social media.

Managing vaccine delivery

As vaccines arrived, the Government of Somalia, the regional WHO team, and Dimagi launched a CommCare system to manage COVID-19 vaccine delivery nationwide. Going live on March 20, 2021, it let frontline health workers track each individual before, during, and after vaccination, while stakeholders monitored delivery through custom dashboards updated in real time. Because it was built on CommCare, the same system could later be adapted to manage routine immunization and other services, an example of digital infrastructure outlasting the emergency that created it.

Scaling up rapid testing

Testing was the other half of the response. FIND, the global alliance for diagnostics and co-convener of the diagnostics pillar of the Access to COVID-19 Tools (ACT) Accelerator, contracted Dimagi to build a digital monitoring and evaluation tool for antigen rapid diagnostic test (Ag RDT) trainings across up to 15 low- and middle-income countries. The tool tracked training and test uptake for 5,000 users through key performance indicators, giving programme managers and country partners the data to improve training and strengthen the quality of Ag RDT testing.

Together, these apps reflect a single lesson from Dimagi’s pandemic response: digital solutions are far more effective when they are open, adaptable, and ready before an emergency begins. Each was built to be reused, and much of this work now lives on in tools for routine immunization, community health, and outbreak preparedness.
