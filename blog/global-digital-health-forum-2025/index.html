Join Dimagi at the Global Digital Health Forum 2025

Dimagi returns to GDHF to connect with partners, share insights from our work, and explore the innovations shaping the future of digital health.

SS
Sarah Strauss Oct 2025 5 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Dimagi returns to the Global Digital Health Forum in 2025.

The Global Digital Health Forum (GDHF) is always a highlight on the Dimagi calendar, and we’re thrilled to be returning this year to connect with partners, share insights from our work, and explore the innovations shaping the future of digital health.

This year’s forum comes at a defining moment for our sector. Unprecedented funding contractions have disrupted digital health efforts around the world, threatening progress on health outcomes but also reminding us why this work, and this gathering, matter more than ever. Out of that disruption comes a chance to rebuild, placing equity, local ownership, and meaningful outcomes at the heart of digital health, shifting from funding effort to paying for outcomes.

Connect with us at GDHF

If you’re attending in person, stop by our booth to meet the Dimagi team. See demos of CommCare, our flagship platform for impactful frontline work, and CommCare Connect, which enables frontline workers to learn new skills, deliver verified services, and earn income for their impact. Learn more about Open Chat Studio for building public health chatbots, or how SureAdhere supports virtual patient engagement for complex treatment regimens.

Whether you’re interested in our products, exploring partnership opportunities, or simply want to reconnect, we’d love to see you there.

Appy Hour with Dimagi and Living Goods

This year, GDHF’s traditional Happy Hour is getting a digital twist with Appy Hour. Dimagi has a long history of hosting community Happy Hours at GDHF, and we’re excited to continue that tradition in partnership with Living Goods. Join us on December 3 at 5pm for an informal showcase of app presentations, conversation, and connection.

Presentations and sessions from Dimagi

CATSCoach: lessons from AI coaching for frontline workers

CATSCoach is an AI-powered coaching tool developed by Dimagi and Zvandiri to strengthen the counseling skills of Community Adolescent Treatment Supporters in Zimbabwe. Built on Open Chat Studio and structured around the EQUIP framework, it enabled 100 workers to practice in English, Shona, and Ndebele, even offline. Over half of reviewed users improved in at least one skill domain, and 82% found the tool helpful.

Evaluating multi-language GenAI chatbots for sexual and reproductive health

This panel brings together stakeholders evaluating GenAI-powered chatbots used by young people in Kenya and Senegal, exploring model safety, cultural relevance, user experience, and health outcomes, and asking what it truly takes to know whether LLM-powered chatbots deliver real-world impact.

“They called us”: community-driven KMC enrollment

A frontline worker and two locally led organizations share how community-based Kangaroo Mother Care in Uganda, delivered through CommCare Connect, has enrolled over 675 newborns, facilitated 1,400 home visits, and disbursed more than $20,000 in payments. The most telling outcome: communities now call workers directly to enroll newborns.

Forging consensus on common mental health measures

A lightning talk on findings from a global survey by Dimagi with Grand Challenges Canada, Friendship Bench, and 32 organizations across five continents, calling for a shift beyond symptom tracking toward holistic measures of psychosocial well-being and resilience.
