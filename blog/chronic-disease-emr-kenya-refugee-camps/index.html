Electronic Medical Records for Chronic Disease Care in Kenyan Refugee Camps

A peer-reviewed evaluation of a purpose-built, CommCare-integrated NCD electronic medical record in Kenya’s Hagadera refugee camp.

J
JMIR mHealth & uHealth October 2023 4 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Dadaab, the refugee-camp complex in northern Kenya that includes Hagadera, where the NCD electronic medical record runs on CommCare. Photo: Pete Lewis / DFID (CC BY 2.0).

Cross-posting this resource — a peer-reviewed study by Lyles et al., published in JMIR mHealth and uHealth (October 2023). Read the original article. The highlights below focus on the role of CommCare.

A purpose-built electronic medical record for non-communicable diseases (NCDs), integrated with CommCare, was evaluated in Kenya’s Hagadera refugee camp and compared against a general electronic medical record system. The specialized app dramatically improved how consistently chronic-disease care was documented and followed up.

Over an 11-month period (May 2021 to March 2022), the evaluation analyzed records from 1,539 patients at the Hagadera clinic. Patients were predominantly female (60.3%), with an average age of 53; diagnoses included hypertension only (58.3%), diabetes only (23.2%), and both conditions (18.5%).

The specialized NCD application was integrated with CommCare, the platform already used by the International Rescue Committee. The authors note that building on CommCare facilitated adoption and supported future scalability across humanitarian settings.

Documentation was markedly more complete with the purpose-built app. Blood pressure was recorded in 100% of cases, compared with 75% in the general EMR, and the next visit date was scheduled in 99.6% of records, compared with just 14.7%, a substantial improvement in the program’s ability to track continuity of care.

Clinic staff reported that the app standardized patient information and made consultations easier.

“The traceability of the data and the way the data is saved” stood out as a significant improvement over paper records.

Clinic provider, Hagadera

For programs delivering chronic-disease care in low-resource and humanitarian settings, the study offers practical evidence that a fit-for-purpose, CommCare-based NCD tool can raise data quality and follow-up reliability well above what a generic EMR achieves.
