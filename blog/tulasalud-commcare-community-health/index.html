TulaSalud: CommCare for Improving and Monitoring Community Health

How TulaSalud used CommCare to improve and monitor community health in Guatemala, supporting frontline health workers and strengthening maternal and child care across four rural regions.

D
Dimagi Jan 2015 3 min read
Share LinkedIn X

A TulaSalud frontline health worker delivering community care in rural Guatemala.

Guatemala’s northern highlands carry one of the highest burdens of maternal mortality in the world, driven by widespread poverty and a severe lack of formal health services. To improve maternal care and reproductive health, TulaSalud introduced a digital health program for frontline workers powered by CommCare, working alongside the Guatemalan Ministry of Health to extend care into the country’s most remote communities.

High risk, no digital infrastructure

Rural Guatemala suffers from a severe shortage of formal health services. TulaSalud focuses on four regions with extremely high rates of maternal mortality, child malnutrition, and malaria. Many pregnancies in these regions are considered high-risk, and the children who are born often suffer from chronic malnutrition. To reach a population of nearly 3.4 million people, 72% of whom live below the poverty line, TulaSalud needed a solution that could deliver the care these communities deserve.

The organization had already introduced a digital health platform, but it fell short in the field: non-healthcare-specific workflows, poor usage in rural areas, limited data flow and decision support, and no case management.

Kawok, a CommCare-based app built for the field

Seeking to replace their original digital health solution, TulaSalud turned to CommCare. Their goal was for a new application, called Kawok and built on CommCare, to strengthen the Ministry of Health’s system of care by expanding the network of app-enabled frontline workers and increasing accountability at every level through better supervision.

Kawok works as both a job aid and a monitoring platform. For frontline workers, it improves productivity in maternal and neonatal care, community surveying, and malaria monitoring and treatment, and offers offline access to 38 health education videos translated into 8 Mayan languages. For program administrators, it evaluates the productivity of frontline workers, tracks individual high-risk pregnancies, and helps prioritize limited resources in the highest-risk communities.

TulaSalud’s IT team built Kawok as a suite of tools on the CommCare platform, combining custom application development, reporting tools, and Google Earth. Staff can look up patient locations and health information on customized dashboards and maps, and monitor disease outbreaks in real time. Frontline workers also use the app to log the phone calls they make to patients, supervisors, and health clinics, a program that places more than 10,000 calls each week.

From one district pilot to four regions

Kawok’s initial rollout began in the district of Senahu, where Dimagi and TulaSalud ran interviews and user tests with a small group of frontline workers who had been using CommCare in the field for six weeks. Teams from both organizations also shadowed workers on patient visits to see how the application performed in practice.

After an effective pilot covering roughly one-third of Alta Verapaz, TulaSalud expanded coverage to the entire region, introducing the application to more than 4,000 nurses, frontline workers, and supervisors. New features followed, including modules for specific health crises and phone-call tracking, and the program scaled to three additional regions: Huehuetenango, Quiché, and Sololà.

Results across 700 communities

Using the application, frontline workers have logged more than 300,000 cases across 700 communities that together account for more than 3.4 million people. Services can be delivered in five Mayan indigenous languages, ensuring no one goes without care.

  • 3.4 million people reached across 700 communities in rural Guatemala.
  • 22,000 pregnancies logged and tracked through the Kawok app.
  • 81,500 malnourished children identified and supported through the program.
Cases logged in the field
300,000+

Frontline workers have logged more than 300,000 cases through Kawok, including 81,500 malnourished children and 22,000 pregnancies, across 700 communities in rural Guatemala.

The impact reaches beyond raw numbers. Thanks to integrated care-delivery modules and immediate in-app feedback, frontline workers were able to spot risk factors in over 54% of the pregnancies they tracked. TulaSalud and the local Ministry of Health now monitor the performance of more than 4,000 frontline health workers and nurses, offering guidance to keep improving the quality of care.

“CommCare is a great mHealth platform that is very well designed, implemented, continually improved and supported. We could not have developed Kawok without CommCare.”

Ray Brunsting, CTO, Tula Foundation
