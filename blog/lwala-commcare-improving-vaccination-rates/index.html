Lwala Community Alliance: CommCare for Improving Vaccination Rates

How Lwala Community Alliance reached 95% vaccination coverage across 60,000 people in western Kenya and cut child mortality from 105 to 29.5 per 1,000 live births with a custom CommCare app.

D
Dimagi Sep 2019 4 min read Case Study
Share LinkedIn X

A Lwala community health worker visiting a household in western Kenya.

Lwala Community Alliance fights high rates of maternal mortality and HIV in western Kenya through a community-led health model. Using a custom CommCare mobile application, its Community Health Workers (CHWs) register every pregnant woman and child under five into the formal healthcare system, then follow a guided workflow for vaccinations, family planning, disease diagnosis, and more. So far the program has reached vaccination rates above 95% across a population of 60,000 people.

A compounded health crisis

In 2007, western Kenya faced the compounded crises of rampant HIV transmission and drastically high maternal and child mortality. 82 children under five died per 1,000 live births, and HIV prevalence ran between 16–20%, compared to a national rate of 6%. Early healthcare initiatives relied on paper, but lags in data submission, typically up to a month, stifled efforts to provide quality care.

Those delays carried real consequences:

  • Delayed data. Submission lags of up to a month made it impossible to identify and act on the lowest-coverage communities in time.
  • Missed immunizations. Without automated reminders, children who had partially missed their vaccination schedule were easy to lose track of across a dispersed rural population.
  • Fragmented care. CHWs had no integrated tool to manage vaccination, family planning, pregnancy tracking, and HIV follow-up in a single workflow, each required separate paper forms.

Lwala Mobile, a custom app built on CommCare

Lwala began its CHW initiative in 2012 with a paper-based system that fed data into Excel and eventually Salesforce. After delays of up to a month, the organization moved to mobile data collection, evaluating its options and choosing CommCare for its powerful, customizable functionality that the team could develop themselves rather than relying on a vendor.

The resulting Lwala Mobile app registers every household, walks the CHW through the correct workflow for vaccination, family planning, or other services, and integrates directly with Salesforce to upload all data instantly. That data can then be analyzed to pinpoint the geographies with the lowest vaccination rates and begin the process again in those areas. Program administrators track CHW performance against specific indicators, and the same data is easily shared with the Ministry of Health to support policy change conversations.

“Lwala’s CHWs are able to track children who are not fully immunized in CommCare and it prompts them to remind mothers to take their children to receive the remaining vaccinations. This is so important because children who are not fully immunized may be susceptible to permanent disabilities.”

Anthony Maina, Systems Administrator, Lwala Community Alliance

Making sure no child slips through the cracks

The app features a module called “Children Not Fully Immunized,” where a CHW can pull up every child in their assigned households who still needs a vaccination (OPV 0, OPV 1, OPV 2, OPV 3, and Measles 18). A CHW supervisor can see the combined list across all of their CHWs and follow up to ensure compliance.

When a CHW opens a child’s immunization page, she sees the full vaccination history and the dates already recorded. The app also prompts her as the child reaches each milestone. When a child passes two weeks old, for example, the page displays a notification: “The child is now over 2 weeks old. Please enter their OPV 0 date.” This combination of tracking and supervision is what keeps every child on schedule.

Vaccination coverage
95%

Lwala has immunized 95% of children across its communities, 67% higher than the regional average, by tracking every child’s vaccination schedule in CommCare and prompting follow-up before any are lost to follow-up.

Measurable change across the program

Moving from paper to a mobile-first, data-driven model transformed outcomes across Lwala’s communities:

  • 95% vaccination coverage, 67% higher than the regional average.
  • Child mortality down from 105 to 29.5 per 1,000 live births.
  • 96% of mothers deliver in a health facility, versus a regional average of 53% and a 2010 baseline of 26%.
  • 98% prevention of mother-to-child HIV transmission.

The program now covers all of North and East Kamagambo, providing healthcare to 60,000 Kenyans, and Lwala is working to influence the way more than one million people access healthcare.

“Through the Lwala Mobile app, CHWs have maintained a 95% immunization rate. The app also eases the CHWs’ workload via the Task Module to simply track referrals and follow-ups for their priority clients.”

John Oyugi, CHW Supervisor, Lwala Community Alliance
