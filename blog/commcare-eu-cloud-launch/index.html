Choose Where Your CommCare Data Lives with Our New EU Cloud

CommCare’s EU Cloud launches in Ireland, joining India and the US, so you can choose your data’s region with identical features, pricing, and security.

GJ
Gillian Javetski Sep 2025 3 min read
Share LinkedIn X

CommCare adds EU (Ireland) hosting, alongside India and the US.

Your data, your choice. Starting today, every new CommCare project can be hosted in one of three secure regions, the European Union (Ireland), India (Mumbai), or the US (Virginia), at exactly the same price and with the same feature set.

CommCare cloud regions
3 regions

EU (Ireland), India (Mumbai), and US (Virginia), with identical features, pricing, and enterprise-grade security, so you can keep data where your policies, funders, or team prefer.

Why give teams this choice?

Frontline programs operate in vastly different legal and operational contexts. Offering region-level data residency builds trust and puts control in your hands. All three cloud hosting locations run on SOC 2-certified AWS infrastructure with enterprise-grade security, full GDPR and HIPAA compliance, and end-to-end encryption.

This expansion gives organizations that prefer or need their data hosted in the EU an option that makes no compromises on functionality. Mobile and web apps, offline capability, integrations, and support remain exactly the same worldwide: open-source transparency delivered as a secure, fully managed cloud service, without the maintenance burden.

Getting started

New to CommCare? You can sign up for an account on the EU Cloud or the India Cloud in minutes.

Already using CommCare? Because cloud locations are isolated, project data can’t be migrated between regions. For full details on CommCare’s cloud hosting locations, visit this page.

Looking ahead

This launch is one more step in our commitment to removing barriers to digitally enabling frontline work. Earlier this year we introduced a $100/month entry-level plan; today we’re expanding hosting choice so every organization, whether in Nairobi, New Delhi, or the Netherlands, has greater control over where its data lives.
