Zvandiri Connect: Transforming Youth-Led Health Services Through Digital Innovation

How Zvandiri built Zvandiri Connect, a youth-centred digital health platform on Dimagi’s CommCare, equipping 950 peer counsellors across nine provinces to support more than 60,000 young people living with HIV in Zimbabwe.

Z
Zvandiri December 2025 3 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Zvandiri Connect brings counselling workflows, case files, and data systems into one CommCare application for peer counsellors.

This post was copied from Zvandiri’s original post, which can be found here.

Zvandiri has always placed connection at the centre of its work, training and mentoring young people living with HIV to become peer counsellors. These young leaders, known as Community Adolescent Treatment Supporters (CATS) and Young Mentor Mothers (YMMs), are the heartbeat of Zvandiri’s model. As governments across the region scale peer-led health services, Zvandiri has continued to innovate to ensure these young people have the tools they need to deliver high-quality, compassionate care.

In 2024, this commitment led to the launch of Zvandiri Connect, a youth-centred digital health platform built on Dimagi’s CommCare system. Zvandiri Connect brings together all the tools, guides, counselling workflows and data systems previously spread across paper forms and separate resources, combining them into one easy-to-use digital application. For the first time, peer counsellors can access complete case files, screening tools, counselling content, videos, referral pathways and follow-up reminders directly from a single device.

The shift has transformed the work of peer counsellors. Instead of juggling fragmented information, CATS and YMMs now work with integrated digital case management tools that streamline planning, improve accuracy and strengthen continuity of care. From HIV adherence support and viral load monitoring to mental health screening, TB prevention, sexual and reproductive health and caregiver engagement, every service is now connected.

This integration has not only improved quality, it has enabled scale. In just months, Zvandiri deployed the platform across nine provinces, equipping 950 peer counsellors to support more than 60,000 children, adolescents and young adults from 480 sites in 43 districts. The rapid rollout has strengthened national systems, improved service efficiency and reduced the burden of paperwork for young providers, giving them more time to focus on what matters most, connecting with their peers.

The scale of the work is important, but the impact is felt by the young people and families reached. Caregivers receive timely reminders, young people get information that makes sense to them, and peer counsellors feel confident and supported.
