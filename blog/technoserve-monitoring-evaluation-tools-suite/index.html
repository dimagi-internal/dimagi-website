TechnoServe: Building a Monitoring & Evaluation Tools Suite

How TechnoServe worked with Dimagi to build a suite of CommCare-based monitoring and evaluation tools supporting its programs across Africa and Latin America.

D
Dimagi Sep 2020 3 min read Case Study
Share LinkedIn X

TechnoServe field teams capture program data on CommCare across Africa and Latin America.

TechnoServe has worked to create impact in more than 30 countries for over 50 years. In the last five years alone, it has reached almost half a million beneficiaries across Latin America, Africa, and India, a scale that demanded an impressive combination of expertise and flexibility from its monitoring and evaluation (M&E) infrastructure.

A single M&E platform for 30 countries and counting

TechnoServe runs agricultural extension, cooperative development, and small business advisory programs simultaneously across dozens of countries, each with distinct data requirements and indicator sets. To service those needs around the world, the organization re-organized its team structure and compiled a suite of products built on CommCare that let it capture, manage, and analyze data from an ever-changing set of indicators.

The result is a reusable, adaptable toolkit that TechnoServe can stand up for any new program without starting from scratch each time. A process that once took six months or more is now achievable in half that time.

Global programs, constantly changing indicators

Standing up a new M&E system from scratch for each program was slow work, too slow for a global NGO responding to new funding cycles and program launches year-round. At the same time, with nearly half a million beneficiaries and field data arriving from multiple continents, maintaining data quality and consistency across programs required systematic validation rather than ad-hoc spreadsheet checks.

TechnoServe needed a foundation flexible enough to serve very different programs, yet structured enough to keep data clean as it scaled.

A reusable suite built on CommCare

TechnoServe’s M&E team used CommCare’s configurability to build a core toolkit of forms, case types, and logic that could be adapted for each new program without rebuilding from the ground up.

  • Case management: Beneficiaries are tracked as longitudinal cases, giving M&E officers a continuous record of enrollment, advisory visits, and outcome measurement over the life of a program.
  • Display conditions: CommCare’s display logic shows or hides form fields based on prior answers, so the same form can serve programs with different indicator sets without exposing irrelevant questions to field workers.
  • Validation conditions: Automated range checks, skip logic, and required-field enforcement catch data entry errors in the field, before bad data reaches the reporting layer.
  • Rapid adaptation: Because the core structure is reusable, the team can configure a new program’s M&E forms in weeks rather than months, matching the pace of program launches.

“A process that would take six months or more is now achievable in half that time. CommCare gives our M&E team a foundation we can adapt to any program we launch, without starting from scratch.”

TechnoServe M&E team

Half a million beneficiaries, half the implementation time

By leaning on a reusable, CommCare-based toolkit, TechnoServe transformed how quickly it can launch and monitor programs across continents. The shift delivered measurable gains in reach, coverage, and speed:

  • Nearly 500,000 beneficiaries reached across Africa, Latin America, and India in the last five years.
  • 30+ countries where TechnoServe has deployed programs on CommCare.
  • Implementation time cut from six-plus months to under three per program launch.
Implementation time
6+ mo → under 3

A reusable CommCare toolkit cut the time to stand up a new program’s M&E system roughly in half, letting TechnoServe match the pace of new funding cycles and launches year-round.

The toolkit is now a foundation TechnoServe can adapt for agricultural extension, cooperative development, or small business advisory work alike, keeping data clean and comparable as the organization continues to scale across new countries and sectors.
