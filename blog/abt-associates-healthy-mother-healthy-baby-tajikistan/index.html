Abt Associates: Healthy Mother Healthy Baby, Tajikistan

Partnering with Abt Associates on a USAID-funded effort to strengthen maternal, newborn, and child health and nutrition services in the Khatlon region of Tajikistan.

D
Dimagi Apr 2022 2 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Dimagi is partnering with Abt Associates on the USAID-funded Healthy Mother Healthy Baby project in Tajikistan.

Dimagi is partnering with Abt Associates in the USAID-funded Healthy Mother Healthy Baby (HMHB) project that aims to establish and augment digital transformation in the Republic of Tajikistan. The Healthy Mother, Healthy Baby (HMHB) Activity expands USAID’s maternal, newborn, and child health (MNCH) and nutrition investments in Tajikistan as the country introduces new health strategies. It is a multi-year project with a mandate to make sustainable improvements in the ability of the Government of Tajikistan to deliver quality maternal and child health and nutrition services. The project focuses on building the technical capacity, leadership, management, and policy reform potential of Tajikistan.

Healthy Mother Healthy Baby aims to improve the quality of healthcare, increase access to health services, and help meet increasing demand in the 12 districts of the Khatlon region in Tajikistan. A suite of applications will be developed as part of the project across a range of areas such as facility assessment, training tracking and supervision, and routine surveys for understanding knowledge, access and practice (KAP) on WASH and nutrition topics, as well as a maternal and child health and nutrition tracking app that supports healthcare workers with antenatal, postpartum and postnatal care service delivery. Since the project focuses on nutrition in an effort to reduce malnutrition during pregnancy and in children under 5 years old, the app will therefore also include gravidogram and growth monitoring components. All the data that is collected using CommCare will be sent to DHIS2 for reporting and visualization, and also to a server at the Republican Center for Medical Statistics and Information to expedite decision-making.

Applications developed for the project

Learn more about the applications developed as part of this project:

  • Knowledge Hub
  • Knowledge, Attitude and Practice Application
  • Continuous Medical Education Application
  • Rapid Health Facility Assessment Application
  • Maternal, Newborn and Child Health Application
  • DHIS2 and CommCare integration
