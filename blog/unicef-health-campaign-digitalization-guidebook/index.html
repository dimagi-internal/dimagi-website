Inside UNICEF’s Digital Tool Selection Guidebook for Health Campaigns

Dimagi’s perspective on UNICEF’s Digital Tool Selection Guidebook for Health Campaigns, which recognizes CommCare as a leading tool for campaign digitalization.

ST
Sarvesh Tewari Aug 2025 5 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Field-tested digital tools for health campaigns across sub-Saharan Africa.

CommCare has been recognized in UNICEF’s Digital Tool Selection Guidebook for Health Campaigns, a newly released resource on health campaign digitalization. Having contributed to the guidebook alongside UNICEF and the Clinton Health Access Initiative over the past year, I’ve gained valuable perspective on where the global health community is heading, and why our approach at Dimagi resonates.

Universal campaign challenges

Whether it’s malaria vector control, mass drug administration, or immunization drives, the fundamental challenges stay consistent. Coverage gaps, workforce coordination, supply chain disruptions, and data quality issues plague campaigns regardless of geography or disease focus, and these interconnected problems require integrated solutions, not piecemeal fixes.

Field-tested at scale

Our experience speaks to this integrated approach.

Indoor residual spraying reach
11M+ households

Across 14 sub-Saharan African countries, we’ve supported indoor residual spraying campaigns for malaria prevention, reaching over 11 million households with more than 1,000 CommCare users.

In Malawi, our cStock system reached 80%+ reporting rates among nearly 4,000 community health workers, transforming supply chain visibility. In Mozambique, we’ve equipped over 1,000 community health workers with integrated case management tools. These deployments taught us that successful campaign digitalization requires offline-first architecture, multilingual support, multimedia for varied literacy levels, and seamless interoperability with systems like DHIS2.

Beyond the technology

Technology alone doesn’t guarantee success. The most sophisticated platform fails without proper implementation support, genuine partnership, and sustained capacity building, a human-centered approach that has always been central to Dimagi’s work.

Security and standards

Campaign digitalization demands uncompromising security. CommCare maintains SOC-2 compliance, HIPAA certification, GDPR compliance, and AES-256 encryption, not checkboxes to tick but commitments to protecting the communities we serve.

Looking forward

CommCare’s recognition in this guidebook affirms that the strongest campaign solutions balance technical sophistication with usability, and robust functionality with flexible customization. To see how our experience in digital health campaigns aligns with the guidebook’s frameworks, explore our work with partners across Africa and beyond.

Planning your next campaign?

Whether it’s a malaria campaign, mass drug administration, or integrated service delivery, we’d welcome a conversation about how CommCare can support your objectives.
