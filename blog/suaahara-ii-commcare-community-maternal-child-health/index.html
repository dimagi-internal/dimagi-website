Suaahara II: CommCare for Community-Based Maternal & Child Health in Nepal

How the seven-year, USAID-funded Suaahara II program used CommCare to register 2 million households and reach 10 million people across 42 of Nepal’s 77 districts.

D
Dimagi Mar 2021 4 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Suaahara II frontline workers use CommCare to register households and counsel mothers across rural Nepal.

Suaahara II (SII) is a seven-year, USAID-funded program that uses a household-based approach to improve the nutrition of pregnant and lactating women and children under two across 42 of Nepal’s 77 districts. Building on lessons from Suaahara I, the consortium chose CommCare to give frontline workers the timely, targeted data they were missing.

Finding the 1,000-day households

The program needed to identify which households were in the critical 1,000-day window between conception and a child’s second birthday, collect regular data on their behaviors, and make it instantly usable for field staff. SII built three connected CommCare applications:

  • Community Mapping Census: a door-to-door roster of every household, used to prioritize visits and send stage-based SMS messages.
  • Monitoring Tools: follows 1,000-day mothers through pregnancy, the first six months, and months 7 to 24, tracking targets like antenatal visits and iron-folic-acid intake.
  • Job Aid: gives community nutrition facilitators situation-appropriate counseling scripts for each household visit.

“CommCare is amazing. It has helped us to collect high-volume information easily and also in the management and monitoring of a huge amount of staff spread across a huge geographical area.”

Bharat Sarma Prasai, Program Coordinator, Suaahara II
Households registered
2 million

Between December 2016 and December 2020, Suaahara II’s 1,500 frontline workers used CommCare to register 2 million households and 10 million Nepali residents, including about 200,000 women in the 1,000-day window.

The investment paid off beyond the numbers. In 2017 SII won the grand prize in USAID’s Innovation to Action, which partners tied directly to its use of CommCare, and when COVID-19 reached Nepal in 2020, the existing database of phone numbers let the program pivot to tele-counseling and SMS to keep care going.
