Dimagi makes the Inc. 5000 list for the tenth time

For the 10th year, Dimagi has been named to the Inc. 5000 list of the fastest-growing private companies in the United States.

AV
Amie Vaccaro Aug 2023 4 min read
Share LinkedIn X

Dimagi was named to the Inc. 5000 list for the 10th time in 2023.

For the 10th year, Dimagi was once again celebrated as part of the Inc. 5000 List of the fastest-growing private companies in the United States. The prestigious ranking provides a data-driven look at the most successful companies within the economy’s most dynamic segment: its independent, entrepreneurial businesses. Facebook, Chobani, Under Armour, Microsoft, Patagonia, and many other household name brands gained their first national exposure as honorees on the Inc. 5000.

“We are encouraged by this achievement and grateful to Inc. for this honor. This is truly a testament to our team’s incredible ability to drive our vision of creating a world where everyone has access to the services they need to thrive.”

Jonathan Jackson, Co-Founder & CEO, Dimagi

Dimagi Co-Founder & CEO Jonathan Jackson shared more on what the recognition means, tying it to the company’s 5 Year Strategy for High-Impact Growth: “At the core of our work is a focus on improving the jobs of Frontline Workers around the world to improve global health and development outcomes, and while revenue growth is essential to keep us sustainable, it’s the growth in our impact that I care most about. Looking ahead to 2023 and beyond, growing our impact to meet the growing need gets more and more critical, so we are grateful to Inc. for shining this spotlight on the important work Dimagi does alongside many incredible partners around the world.”

About the 2023 list

The Inc. 5000 class of 2023 represents companies that have driven rapid revenue growth while navigating inflationary pressure, the rising costs of capital, and seemingly intractable hiring challenges.

Jobs added
1.19M

This year’s Inc. 5000 companies added 1,187,266 jobs to the economy over the past three years.

For complete results of the Inc. 5000, including company profiles and an interactive database that can be sorted by industry, location, and other criteria, go to www.inc.com/inc5000. The top 500 companies are featured in the September issue of Inc. magazine.

“Running a business has only gotten harder since the end of the pandemic,” says Inc. editor-in-chief Scott Omelianuk. “To make the Inc. 5000, with the fast growth that requires, is truly an accomplishment. Inc. is thrilled to honor the companies that are building our future.”

Recent milestones

Since the last Inc. 5000 list announcement, Dimagi has achieved some important milestones:

  • We celebrated 20 years of impact.
  • We launched our High-Impact Growth podcast to share learnings from two decades of building solutions for impact.
  • We received $25 million to develop transformative technology for Frontline Workers from the Steele Foundation for Hope.
  • We became a pledging organization to the Global Fund, with a $5 million pledge.
  • We launched WellMe, a resilience-building application for Frontline Workers.
  • We launched the CommCare Provider Program, a global community of vetted agencies that can provide targeted services to CommCare users.
  • We received support from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation through the Grand Challenges initiative to create an LLM-powered coach to offer much-needed support and supervision to Frontline Workers in low- and middle-income countries.

We look forward to continuing our journey of High-Impact Growth in 2024 and are grateful to Inc. for this honor.

More about Inc. and the Inc. 5000

Methodology

Companies on the 2023 Inc. 5000 are ranked according to percentage revenue growth from 2019 to 2022. To qualify, companies must have been founded and generating revenue by March 31, 2019. They must be U.S.-based, privately held, for-profit, and independent, not subsidiaries or divisions of other companies, as of December 31, 2022. (Since then, some on the list may have gone public or been acquired.) The minimum revenue required for 2019 is $100,000; the minimum for 2022 is $2 million. As always, Inc. reserves the right to decline applicants for subjective reasons. Growth rates used to determine company rankings were calculated to four decimal places.

About Inc.

Inc. Business Media is the leading multimedia brand for entrepreneurs. Through its journalism, Inc. aims to inform, educate, and elevate the profile of our community: the risk-takers, the innovators, and the ultra-driven go-getters who are creating our future. Inc.’s award-winning work reaches more than 50 million people across a variety of channels, including events, print, digital, video, podcasts, newsletters, and social media. Its proprietary Inc. 5000 list, produced every year since 1982, analyzes company data to rank the fastest-growing privately held businesses in the United States. The recognition that comes with inclusion on this and other prestigious Inc. lists, such as Female Founders and Power Partners, gives the founders of top businesses the opportunity to engage with an exclusive community of their peers, and credibility that helps them drive sales and recruit talent.

For more information, visit www.inc.com. For more information on the Inc. 5000 Conference & Gala, slated for October 31 to November 2 in San Antonio, visit conference.inc.com.
