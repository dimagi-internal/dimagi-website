Inside Volunteers of America’s Tech-Enabled Approach to Community Health Care

How Volunteers of America equipped its Community Health Worker workforce with CommCare to address social determinants of health, and what it takes to sustain community-based care.

AM
Alberto Maldonado, Volunteers of America Apr 2025 5 min read
Share LinkedIn X

AmeriCorps Community Health Worker Jesus Nieves speaks with Dimagi staff in Puerto Rico, March 2025.

Volunteers of America (VOA) is one of the nation’s largest and most established human services organizations, reaching more than 2 million people annually across 47 states, Washington, D.C., and Puerto Rico. A key part of that work is VOA’s growing Community Health Worker (CHW) workforce, often recruited from the very communities they serve.

Yet despite their critical role, many CHWs lack the tools to efficiently track client progress, coordinate referrals, and demonstrate outcomes. That’s why VOA partnered with Dimagi to equip our CHW workforce with a robust, low-code case management platform built on CommCare, strengthening our ability to address Social Determinants of Health at scale while letting CHWs focus more on client engagement and less on administrative burden.

Addressing the root causes of health disparities

Access to healthcare is about more than doctor’s visits; it’s about addressing the everyday challenges that keep people from staying engaged in their care. As trusted members of the communities they serve, CHWs provide culturally responsive support, help clients navigate complex social and healthcare systems, and advocate for needs traditional providers often can’t. With support from Public Health AmeriCorps, we’re expanding our CHW workforce and investing in digital tools that enhance care coordination.

The communities we serve face some of the most persistent barriers to care:

  • Unstable housing: without secure shelter, clients struggle to maintain health routines, store medications, or attend follow-ups.
  • Food insecurity: limited access to nutritious food exacerbates chronic conditions.
  • Transportation gaps: unreliable transit makes it hard to access care or fill prescriptions.
  • Health literacy: treatment plans, insurance paperwork, and social services can be overwhelming without guidance.

As we scaled, our legacy case management system carried high recurring costs and was ill-suited to a mobile, non-technical workforce, so we sought a modern, user-centered solution.

Transforming service delivery through digital enablement

We partnered with Dimagi to implement CommCare, a HIPAA-compliant, low-code case management platform built for frontline teams.

CHWs equipped across four states
30 CHWs

Across Puerto Rico, Minnesota, Texas, and Kentucky, CHWs now work from a single mobile case management system built for high-need, fast-paced settings.

With CommCare, those teams can:

  • Securely record demographics, medical history, and service needs.
  • Complete structured assessments of social drivers of health, from housing stability to behavioral health and social connectedness.
  • Track client goals, interventions, and follow-ups.
  • Generate real-time reports and integrate with VOA’s PowerBI system for deeper insights.

With an intuitive, mobile-friendly interface, CHWs document and access client information in the field with no technical background required. The rollout followed a focused three-month timeline: user interviews and discovery, configuration and testing of an MVP, training and onboarding, and post-live refinement based on feedback.

“I don’t just collaborate with frontline staff, I focus on connecting services with providers and people, ensuring stakeholders are aligned and needs are met. Strengthening data collection systems is key to delivering quality, sustainable community services.”

Alberto Maldonado, Regional Director, VOA Puerto Rico

Charting the future of CHW programs

Even as we expand, systemic challenges persist: high turnover, fragmented funding, and limited sustainable reimbursement through public and private insurance. Technology is a powerful enabler, but the broader challenges will require continued investment, supportive policy, and collaboration across sectors.

Through our partnership with Public Health AmeriCorps, and by co-presenting at conferences and sharing learnings across the field, VOA and Dimagi are contributing to the growing recognition of CHWs as essential partners in advancing health equity, prevention, and community-centered care, a model for transforming health systems from the ground up.
