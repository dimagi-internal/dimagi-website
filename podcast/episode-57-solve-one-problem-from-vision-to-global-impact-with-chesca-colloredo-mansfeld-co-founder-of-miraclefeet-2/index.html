About this episode

One in 800 babies are born with clubfoot, a treatable impairment that, left untreated, prevents a child from walking and becomes a root cause of illiteracy, malnutrition, and poverty in many parts of the world. The condition is routinely treated at birth in high income countries, but children born in low and middle income countries have historically lacked access to quality care.

Chesca Colloredo-Mansfeld co-founded MiracleFeet in 2010, driven by the idea that a newly accepted, low-cost, non-surgical treatment, the Ponseti method, could have an outsized impact. Co-hosts Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro talk with her about leaving the corporate world to become a social entrepreneur, raising money for a problem no one had heard of, and the role data and CommCare played in proving impact and earning a GiveWell grant.

Today MiracleFeet is the largest global organization solely dedicated to ending clubfoot as a solvable problem. The team is on track to serve 100,000 children, with a goal of treating 70% of clubfoot cases in the countries where they work.
