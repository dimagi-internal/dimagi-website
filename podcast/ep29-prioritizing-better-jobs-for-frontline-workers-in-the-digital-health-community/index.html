Prioritizing Better Jobs for Frontline Workers in the Digital Health Community

About this episode

Back from the 2022 Global Digital Health Forum in Washington, DC, John Jackson and Amie Vaccaro reflect on a week of reconnecting with teammates, ministries, and funders, and on one thing that stood out for the wrong reason: how few frontline workers were actually represented in the room.

John walks through the session he led, "Is the Digital Health Community Creating Better Jobs for Users?", and poses a question to the field. Alongside the existing Principles for Digital Development, what if there was a 10th principle to ensure the technology we roll out is creating better jobs, and how would we even measure that? The conversation digs into the tension between accountability tools that serve supervisors and programs and the autonomy and trust that workers actually want.

Grounded in Dimagi's five year strategy of improving jobs to improve outcomes, the episode closes on a practical test: put yourself in the worker's shoes and ask whether a new tool would leave you feeling empowered or disempowered. John and Amie also commit to bringing more community health worker and frontline voices onto the podcast.

“Are we at the end of the day making providers jobs better and are they then able to support their clients better?”

Jonathan Jackson, on what the field too often overlooks

In this episode

  • A 10th principle: John's proposal to add "make the user's job better" to the Principles for Digital Development
  • The missing voice: why frontline workers were so underrepresented at the Global Digital Health Forum
  • Accountability vs. trust: how real-time data tools can serve the supervisor while leaving the worker feeling watched
  • Net effect over perfection: weighing trade-offs so a project's total impact is still a better job
  • Maintenance matters: bad phones, lapsed airtime, and stale documentation signal that an employer doesn't value the worker's time
  • Empowered or disempowered: a simple test of putting yourself in the worker's shoes before rolling out a tool
  • Bringing the voice in: Dimagi's commitment to feature more frontline and community health worker perspectives on the show
