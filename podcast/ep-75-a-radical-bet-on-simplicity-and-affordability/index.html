A Radical Bet on Simplicity and Affordability

After the 2025 USAID cuts, Dimagi Managing Directors Gillian Javetski and Ismaïla Diene on the ‘diamonds’ forged under pressure, and a radical bet on simple, affordable tools.

About this episode

In early 2025, massive funding cuts from USAID sent shockwaves through the global development sector, leaving many to wonder what would come next. The money was gone, but the problems remained. Dimagi Managing Directors Gillian Javetski and Ismaïla Diene rejoin the podcast to talk honestly about the aftermath, and the unexpected diamonds that pressure can produce.

They make the case for radical simplicity and affordability as a design philosophy rather than a compromise, and share how that thinking is shaping the next generation of community health information systems (eCHIS), built to be big enough, simple enough, and cheap enough to last.

It is a frank look at building durable tools when budgets shrink and the stakes stay high.

In this episode

  • The aftermath of the 2025 USAID funding cuts
  • The ‘diamonds’ created under financial pressure
  • Why simplicity and affordability are a strategy, not a sacrifice
  • The next generation of eCHIS
  • Designing tools that are big enough, simple enough, and cheap enough
