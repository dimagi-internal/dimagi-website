About this episode

In part one of a two-part series with Lwala Community Alliance, Jonathan Jackson sits down with Co-CEO Julius Mbeya to explore how a community-led health model is reshaping care in the parts of Kenya that carry a disproportionate burden of maternal and under-five deaths. Julius shares his own story, growing up in Western Kenya among the same health challenges Lwala now works to address, and what drew him to an organization founded by two young people determined to change what they saw.

He lays out the four pillars of Lwala's community-led health model: empowering community committees, professionalizing and equipping community health workers, improving quality at health facilities in partnership with Kenya's Ministry of Health, and generating data to demonstrate impact. Rather than spreading thin, Lwala goes deep where the need is greatest, then uses what it proves locally to inform national policy, from the non-pneumatic anti-shock garment for postpartum hemorrhage to a government-owned electronic community health information system.

Julius also describes how technology transformed the work. Moving community health workers off bulky paper records and onto CommCare by Dimagi gave Lwala near real-time data, individualized longitudinal tracking of clients, and workers who can prioritize the households that need them most.
