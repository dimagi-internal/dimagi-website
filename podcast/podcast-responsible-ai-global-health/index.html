Beyond “AI for Good”: Building Responsible AI

UC Berkeley's Genevieve Smith on why ‘AI for good’ is not enough, and the five lenses every team needs to deploy AI responsibly.

  • Research & Academic
  • AI

About this episode

AI is moving fast in global health and development, but good intentions alone do not guarantee good outcomes. Genevieve Smith, Founding Director of the Responsible AI Initiative at UC Berkeley's AI Research Lab, joins the show to unpack what it really means to build and deploy AI responsibly in the contexts that matter most.

Drawing on a decade in international development and cutting-edge research on AI bias, Genevieve lays out five critical lenses, fairness, privacy, security, transparency, and accountability, that program managers and product leaders must apply to avoid reinforcing existing inequalities or creating new risks.

With real examples, from AI-driven credit assessments that unintentionally disadvantage women to the challenge of generative AI in low-resource and multilingual settings, she points toward emerging alternatives like data cooperatives that give communities governance over how their data is used, shifting power toward trust, agency, and long-term impact.

In this episode

  • Why labeling a project ‘AI for good’ is not enough
  • Five lenses: fairness, privacy, security, transparency, accountability
  • How AI bias shows up, from credit scoring to language gaps
  • The challenge of generative AI in low-resource, multilingual settings
  • Data cooperatives and shifting power back to communities
