Nick Martin of TechChange on Training, Content, and the Evolution and Maturation of Digital Health

Recorded on the sidelines of the 2022 Global Digital Health Forum, Nick Martin of TechChange joins Jonathan Jackson to trace a decade of digital health maturing, and why great training, content, and shared buy-in decide whether tools actually work.

About this episode

Recorded live at the 2022 Global Digital Health Forum in Arlington, Virginia, the first in-person gathering of the global digital health network after COVID, this conversation kicks off a series of sideline interviews Jonathan Jackson recorded with peers across the ecosystem.

Jonathan sits down with Nick Martin, CEO of TechChange, a fellow social enterprise that has spent about 12 years building training, content, and convening for the global development community. They reflect on how the field has shifted from making the case for mHealth to helping ministries of health plan whole, interoperable national systems, including the digital health planning course TechChange built with USAID and PATH.

The two dig into why dry, technical topics like governance and budgeting can still be taught in engaging ways, why the content layer running on top of platforms is the real user experience, and why a shared definition of success years into a project matters as much as the technology itself.

“We are no longer making the case that there should be a field of digital health. We have tons of practitioners who have built incredible tools and ecosystems. So the last few years have really been about connecting those dots and thinking intentionally about the next level.”

Nick Martin, on the maturation of digital health

In this episode

  • A decade of maturation: how the field moved from explaining what mHealth is to helping ministries plan whole, coherent national systems
  • Making the unsexy engaging: TechChange's board game and applied exercises for teaching governance, interoperability, and budgeting
  • The USAID and PATH course for ministries of health, born four years earlier in Lesotho with practitioners who had never spoken across their own governments
  • Content as the user experience: why the layer running on top of CommCare, DHIS2, or OpenMRS decides whether anything works
  • Cohorts over MOOCs: betting on relationship-driven, outcomes-focused learning instead of massive enrollment numbers
  • Scaling without losing quality: a train the trainers model and translations into French, Spanish, and beyond
  • Defining shared success: why year-five sustainability questions should be framed by expected impact, not just budget
