About this episode

Jonathan Jackson sits down with Jacaranda Health’s co-executive directors Nick Pearson & Sathy Rajasekharan at the Global Digital Health Forum, to discuss challenges and learnings along their journey to impact in maternal and child health in Kenya.

Some topics covered in this conversation include:

Jacaranda Health partners with governments to deploy affordable and scalable solutions through government hospitals, where the majority of underserved mothers and babies receive care. In Kenya, they work with the National Ministry of Health and with 20 Kenyan County Governments, with solutions deployed across 1,100+ hospitals and health centers.

Show Notes

“80% of mothers in countries like Kenya are delivering in government hospitals. By setting up our own chain of private hospitals, we were having impact, but the opportunity was really with the public health system.”

Nick Pearson, on the pivot to government partnership

In this episode

  • From hospitals to the public system: why Jacaranda spun out its maternity business to partner with county governments where most underserved mothers receive care
  • Prompts, the gestationally specific text platform that lets moms ask questions and routes danger signs to the nearest facility
  • In-facility mentorship: rebuilding a broken training model so nurse midwives practice and refresh life-saving skills like neonatal resuscitation
  • Amplifying mothers' voices: turning hundreds of thousands of feedback responses into quality-improvement data for government health managers
  • Machine learning with a human in the loop: intent classification, confidence scoring, and automated responses for middle-of-the-night danger-sign questions
  • Communicating a pivot: bringing the board, funders, and the market along when an organization changes direction
  • Beyond Kenya: early work deploying Prompts in Tanzania and Ghana to support immunization and triage
