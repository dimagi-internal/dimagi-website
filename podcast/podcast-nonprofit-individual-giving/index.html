Thinking About Individual Giving? Start Here

Donate.ly's Javan Van Gronigen on why storytelling beats technology, the ‘random acts of marketing’ trap, and building individual giving that scales.

About this episode

As traditional funding sources shift, many nonprofits are asking a hard question: can individual giving become a meaningful and sustainable revenue stream? Javan Van Gronigen, founder of Donate.ly, sits down with Amie Vaccaro and Jonathan Jackson to unpack what it really takes to make that work.

Drawing on more than a decade in digital fundraising, Javan argues that storytelling, not technology, is a nonprofit's most critical asset, and explains why so many organizations sabotage themselves by committing what he calls random acts of marketing. The conversation explores how to move from one-to-one fundraising toward scalable one-to-many models without burning a limited budget, and why testing small bets is essential.

They also dig into the evolving role of AI in fundraising, from speeding up content creation and analysis to raising new questions about authenticity and trust. It is a grounded, honest roadmap for a more crowded and uncertain funding landscape.
