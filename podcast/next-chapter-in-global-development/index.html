Disrupted, Not Defeated: The Next Chapter in Global Development

Devex's Raj Kumar on a post-peak-ODA world, why value-for-money is now survival, and how organizations can shape what comes next.

About this episode

Raj Kumar, President and Editor-in-Chief of Devex, returns to High-Impact Growth for a candid read on where global development goes after a wave of sudden aid cuts and structural overhauls. With co-hosts Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro, he separates the noise from the signal.

They explore the fallout from peak Official Development Assistance and what it means for program funding, why value-for-money has shifted from a nice-to-have to a survival imperative, and how social enterprises can adapt to a world increasingly led by multilateral development banks, development finance institutions, and outcome-based models.

It is essential listening for anyone, from social entrepreneurs and INGO leaders to funders and policymakers, trying to shape the future of development funding before it hardens.

  • The fallout from peak ODA and what it means for program funding
  • Why value-for-money is now a survival imperative
  • Adapting to a world led by MDBs, DFIs, and outcome-based models
  • The future of localization
