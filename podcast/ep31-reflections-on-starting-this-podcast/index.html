Reflections on Starting this Podcast

Co-hosts Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro pause to look back on nine months of High Impact Growth: why they started, what surprised them, the tools they reached for, and what they would tell anyone thinking about launching a podcast.

About this episode

After nine months of recording, co-hosts Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro turn the microphone on themselves. Prompted by a listener request, they check in on why they launched High Impact Growth in May 2022, why they keep going, and what they have learned along the way.

The conversation traces the journey from idea to launch in roughly 30 days, the goal of sharing non-obvious insights rather than chasing a large audience, and the moments that made it feel worthwhile, including a peer who made certain episodes required listening for her team. They also name the people behind the scenes and the tools, like Riverside and Descript, that made the process work.

Along the way they get honest about the tensions of podcasting: when to share an idea early versus waiting until it is proven, the push to bring in more outside voices, and the value of a bias toward shipping. They close with candid encouragement for anyone considering starting their own show.

“If you are interested in starting a podcast, do it. I really recommend it. Find your non-obvious perspectives to share. We need more honest, transparent dialogue.”

Amie Vaccaro, on advice for new podcasters

In this episode

  • From idea to launch in 30 days: how a long-talked-about idea finally became a real show
  • Non-obvious over big numbers: why the goal was a focused niche, not a huge audience
  • Signals it was working: external feedback, funders tuning in, and episodes becoming required listening for a peer's team
  • A healthy tension: sharing ideas early versus waiting until they are proven out
  • Bias to shipping: getting episodes out the door instead of perfecting them forever
  • The toolkit: how Riverside, Descript, and Descript's studio sound shaped the production
  • Where it goes next: bringing in more outside voices, including frontline workers
