About this episode

Recorded at the Global Digital Health Forum 2022, this episode brings Jonathan Jackson together with Olukunle Akinwusi, Technical Officer for Digital Access at FIND, the Global Alliance for Diagnostics, and Erica Troncoso, Technical Advisor and Portfolio Lead for Frontier Technology Solutions at Jhpiego. The conversation centers on how thoughtful diagnostics and digital tools can improve health equity in low and middle income countries.

Akinwusi traces why diagnostics are pivotal to the cascade of care, since people cannot be linked to treatment before they are identified and diagnosed. He describes FIND's work with ministries of health, including adapting rapid antigen testing for decentralized COVID-19 response in Rwanda, and the data cycle of using insights from deployments to inform what to design next.

The discussion returns repeatedly to designing with the end user. Akinwusi explains FIND's reliance on the principles of digital development, the surprises that emerge when health workers repurpose tools in ways the designers never planned, and his guidance for practitioners: engage the country, design alongside the real workflow, and secure genuine buy-in before anything is built.

“It's important that a workflow of the context in which the solution will be used is taken into consideration, because if a solution is designed or developed and it does not align with the workflow where it's gonna be deployed, then the adoption is unlikely.”

Olukunle Akinwusi, on designing for the real workflow

In this episode

  • Diagnostics first: why no one can be linked to treatment before they are identified and diagnosed, and where the biggest diagnostic gaps sit across the cascade of care
  • Decentralizing testing: how FIND worked with the Ministry of Health in Rwanda to adapt rapid antigen testing for improved COVID-19 response
  • A data cycle: using deployment insights to inform what to design next, then feeding new data back into the response
  • Build with the users: FIND's reliance on the principles of digital development to keep solutions aligned to the frontline worker's real workflow
  • Aha moments: when health workers repurposed a patient-monitoring tablet to set their own follow-up reminders, revealing a need no one had designed for
  • Three pivotal steps: engage the country, design alongside the workflow, and secure genuine buy-in before a product is built
  • Defining buy-in: why stakeholders, governments, and partners must agree on what buy-in actually means and how to measure it
