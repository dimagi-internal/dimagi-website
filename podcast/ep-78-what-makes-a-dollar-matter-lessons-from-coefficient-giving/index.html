What Makes a Dollar Matter? Lessons from Coefficient Giving

Coefficient Giving's Deena Mousa on the frameworks behind one of the world's most analytically driven funders, and how to find where a dollar does the most good.

About this episode

What if you could identify exactly where your philanthropic dollar would save the most lives or improve the most years of healthy living? Deena Mousa, lead researcher at Coefficient Giving (formerly Open Philanthropy), walks us through the rigorous frameworks that guide one of the world's most analytically driven funders.

From evaluating causes using importance, tractability, and neglectedness to calculating social return on investment, Deena explains how Coefficient Giving approaches everything from malaria prevention to far less crowded cause areas, an organization that has directed over 4 billion dollars in grants.

It is a clear window into evidence-driven philanthropy, and a useful challenge to how the rest of us think about where to give.
