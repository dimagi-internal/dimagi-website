About this episode

In part three of Dimagi's deep dive into sustaining exponential growth, hosts Amie Vaccaro and Jonathan Jackson talk with Dev More, Senior Director of Product at Dimagi and the person responsible for the CommCare roadmap. The thread running through the conversation is self service: the open source and software as a service model that lets hundreds of organizations build their own CommCare applications, and the backbone Dimagi is betting on to reach a million users.

Dev explains how the biggest growth has come from an enterprise strategy that helps international NGOs institutionalize CommCare across their global operations. The clearest example is the International Rescue Committee, which went from using CommCare in a decentralized way across 15 to 20 countries to standing up a central internal CommCare team, locking in predictable pricing, and giving every country office access to the platform's full capabilities, including deep case management and longitudinal tracking.

Jon and Dev then look ahead at product development: lowering the effort it takes to build high impact applications, investing in a data and analytics ecosystem that serves both self service users and complex custom pipelines, and the discovery that the same enterprise features now help governments scale CommCare nationally across tens of thousands of frontline workers.

“That is one of my bits, to get CommCare to a point where in iNGOs it's as commonplace and central as using email.”

Dev More, on his goal for CommCare

In this episode

  • Self service as the backbone for exponential growth, and why the SaaS model is one of the few places where financial feedback loops actually create more impact
  • Scaling to a million users: how Dev thinks about CommCare as an enterprise platform for data collection and service delivery
  • Inside the IRC rollout: from a decentralized deployment across 15 to 20 countries to a central internal CommCare team and locked in pricing
  • Why case management wins: longitudinal tracking, multi worker caseloads, and the depth that makes CommCare second to none for M and E teams
  • Build once, deploy to many: single sign on, enterprise release management, and lowering the effort per user
  • An 80 to 20 data ecosystem: self service tools for most needs, flexible pipelines for the complex 20 percent, plus integrations with Power BI, Tableau, and DHIS2
  • From global to national: how the same enterprise features help governments scale CommCare across tens of thousands of users
