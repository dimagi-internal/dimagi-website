Today’s episode is part 3 in a series looking back on pivotal time periods in Dimagi’s story in honor of our 20th anniversary. Today we look back at the time between 2010 and 2014 when Dimagi received two rounds of funding from United States Agency for International Development (USAID) Development Innovation Ventures (DIV) program to test CommCare through dozens of pilots in India. This was a time of maximum learning velocity, as we got feedback from users across many organizations and applied it to get better and better every week. Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro are joined by Kriti Mehrotra, Impact Advisor Office of the CEO; Jeremy Wacksman, Global Director of Division Operations; and Sri Ranganathan, Director of Customer Success to unpack the learnings from this time period.

In this episode

  • The DIV mechanism: a stage one grant to equip 10 organizations, then a stage two scale-up to 40 organizations across India
  • The CRS proof point: a Catholic Relief Services maternal and child health project that scaled from 10 pilot users to 285 frontline workers
  • Built with the user's voice, literally, with a community health worker recorded for the app's multimedia counseling on J2ME brick phones
  • Intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation: research on feeding performance data back to health workers in ways that motivate rather than shame
  • Failure is not free: the real cost to users when a project equips them, then drops support, and why adoption is a funnel with a human price
  • Over-invest in training: teaching digital literacy and Hindi typing from scratch, and treating reusable training collateral as core to scale
  • Practices that evolve: weekly field reports and ad hoc communication that fit a small team but had to change on the road to national scale
