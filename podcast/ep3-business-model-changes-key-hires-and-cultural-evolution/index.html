About this episode

In part 2 of the founding story we’ll discuss some key moments in Dimagi’s story such as shifting to open source, launching a software as a service model, and the impact of early hires on the trajectory of the company.

We’ll also hear from Dr. Neal Lesh, Dimagi’s Chief Strategy Officer about why he joined Dimagi and the role Dimagi has played in the mobile health and digital health space over the last 2 decades.

And we’ll explore how Dimagi’s culture has evolved in the last 20 years as the company has grown.  end user

In this episode

  • The sustainability problem: why software impact comes from running a system for years, not from reaching deployment, and how that reframed the business model
  • From open source to SaaS: contributing to platforms like OpenMRS and RapidSMS, then offering CommCare as a low recurring fee to give projects a path to sustainability
  • Engineering for the last mile: how Clayton Sims and Corey Zoo set a culture willing to sacrifice the ideal engineering problem to reach the hardest-to-reach 20 percent of users
  • Aligned from the start: how the early partnership with D-Tree and Neal Lesh, focused on the program rather than donor reports, shaped CommCare
  • The PEPFAR wave: how a surge of US government AIDS funding after 2005 reshaped the digital health landscape Dimagi grew up in
  • Growing up: moving from Jon's dorm room and sprint-mode hustle toward a diverse global team, strong people operations, and a marathon mentality
  • Impact, team, profit: the core ordering Jon says has never changed as the company has scaled
