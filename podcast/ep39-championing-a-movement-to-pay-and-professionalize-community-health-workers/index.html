About this episode

This episode revisits the origins of CommCare in Tanzania, returning to the community health workers who were among its very earliest testers. Amie Vaccaro is joined by Brian DeRenzi, Dimagi's global director of research, and Gayo, who worked with Dimagi in the early days of CommCare in 2008, recruiting and training community health workers to use the application on their phones as they delivered home based care.

Gayo recently went back to the Kiba area in Tanzania and held five conversations with some of those first community health workers, recording and transcribing their reflections on what changed when they moved from paper to a small mobile phone. They discuss the heavy stigma around HIV at the time, how a discreet device protected client privacy where bulky paper registers had drawn attention, and how features like password protection, referral tracking, a demo practice mode, and a supervisor tool emerged directly from the workers' own suggestions.

Running through the conversation is the philosophy Dimagi calls design under the mango tree: sitting with the people you aim to serve, handing them a pen and paper, and letting them shape the tool. As Brian reflects, the technology ultimately boils down to relationships, the trust health workers build with their communities and the trust Gayo built with the health workers themselves.

“These community health workers, they're just people who are not well educated probably, but if you give them a chance, give them a piece of paper and say, how would you like the application to be? They'll just draw down what they think the application should be.”
