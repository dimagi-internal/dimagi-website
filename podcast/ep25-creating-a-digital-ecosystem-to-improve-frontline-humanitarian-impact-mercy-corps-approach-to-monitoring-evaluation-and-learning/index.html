About this episode

Jonathan Jackson sits down with Mercy Corps' Monitoring, Evaluation and Learning (MEL) team: Meri Ghorkhmazyan, Senior Director MEL, Alex Tran, Senior MEL Advisor, and Hanna Camp, Senior Advisor for Technology for MEL. Together they unpack how MEL is the next generation of M&E, adding an explicit learning element that connects data to program design and decision making rather than just accountability reporting.

The team describes the long change management journey Mercy Corps took to fix a deeply disaggregated approach to MEL technology, where country offices ran as many as five to seven overlapping data collection tools with little global guidance or support. Through surveys, interviews, a white paper, and a dedicated task force, they built a recommended suite of private and open source tools, secured enterprise agreements including one for CommCare, and launched training and Jumpstart funds to support adoption.

The conversation also looks ahead to aggregating standardized data across humanitarian responses to surface country, regional, and global trends, and returns again and again to a central lesson: many struggles with technology are really culture, resource, and buy-in issues, so lasting change demands a comprehensive approach, not just better tools.

“Along the way, we found out that sometimes our struggles with technology have nothing to do with the technology. These are culture issues. These are resource issues. These are buy-in issues.”

Meri Ghorkhmazyan, Mercy Corps
