About this episode

A follow-up to Dimagi's earlier conversation on taking a product approach, this episode brings together three product leaders building the company's two most mature offerings, CommCare and SureAdhere. They dig into why global health needs software as a service, and why that model is as challenging as it is important in resource-constrained settings.

The discussion traces the move from project-oriented, donor-driven funding to a product operating model centered on user needs, sustainability, and scale. Along the way the group weighs hard trade-offs between maintaining existing software and chasing new features, the discipline of separating product revenue from project revenue, and the surprisingly central role of product marketing in helping customers see themselves in the product.

Hosted by Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro, the conversation closes with lightning-round lessons on building great products and teams: a clear North Star metric, the power of small focused teams, and good work sustained over a long period of time.

“People don't want a product that can do anything. They want a product that can do their thing. And so, creating those stories that people can recognize themselves in, I think that's a critical missing piece.”

Danny Roberts on product marketing

In this episode

  • Why global health needs SaaS: software you build once and serve many times, where more users lowers the cost per person reached
  • From projects to products: moving off donor-driven, project-based funding into a sustainable product operating model
  • Software rots: why building a product is only the start, and maintenance, security, and new features never stop
  • The maintain vs. build trade-off: making disciplined choices with thin margins and a revenue-sized team
  • Aligning revenue and impact: using a single North Star metric like patient adherence on SureAdhere to guide investment
  • Product marketing as the missing piece: helping a champion's local teams crest the hill and see themselves in CommCare
  • Track product revenue honestly: separating true recurring product revenue from project revenue so you don't fool yourself
