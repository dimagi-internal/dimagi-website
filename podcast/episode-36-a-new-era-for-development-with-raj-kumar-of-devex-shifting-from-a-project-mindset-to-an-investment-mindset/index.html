About this episode

Global development is changing fast under converging social, economic, and political pressures. Jonathan Jackson sits down with Raj Kumar, founder and editor-in-chief of Devex, the independent news platform for development professionals, to unpack why 2023 marks the start of a more challenging era. Growing competition between China, Russia, and the West, a harder set of macroeconomic conditions, and rapidly rising humanitarian needs are all squeezing the money available for long-term development work.

Raj and John explore why getting through this means finding five to ten times more value for money, and why that requires moving away from a project-based way of working toward a more collaborative, results-driven investment mindset. They look at where the new funding will come from, including reformed multilateral development banks and a growing wave of philanthropy, and how technology can unlock the potential of the people closest to the problems. Raj closes with direct messages for technologists and for funders on how to work differently.

“Another word for a bet is an investment. Investments can succeed, they can fail.”

Raj Kumar

In this episode

  • An overnight success that took 23 years: how Devex grew from a grad school project into an independent newsroom doing real talk on the aid sector
  • A new era, not just a new year: the three converging shifts of a new cold war with China and Russia, harder economic conditions, and surging humanitarian needs
  • Where the money goes next: shrinking bilateral aid, multilateral development banks reforming from bankers into deal makers, and philanthropy poised to grow
  • The project mindset problem: why government procurement is naturally project oriented and why that no longer fits the realities on the ground
  • Paying for results, not reports: why this shift is more fundamental than localization for getting five to ten times more value for money
  • Treating failure as learning: framing development work as investments and bets, dropping the ego, and designing for scale from the start
  • Messages for technologists and funders: build for low resource settings the way you would for any market, then listen and fund the people doing it
