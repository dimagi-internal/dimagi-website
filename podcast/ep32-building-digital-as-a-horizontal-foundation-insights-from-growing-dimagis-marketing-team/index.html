About this episode

In this episode Dimagi flips the script: CEO and co-founder Jonathan Jackson interviews co-host Amie Vaccaro, Senior Director of Marketing, about her career journey and the lessons she has learned growing the marketing team at Dimagi. Amie traces her path from studying psychology, through management consulting and social-impact work, to product marketing roles at companies including Salesforce, Change.org, and Autodesk, before joining Dimagi.

The conversation draws a parallel that runs throughout the episode: digital only unlocks its potential for iNGOs and governments when it becomes a foundational, horizontal layer that supports every part of an organization, rather than a siloed vertical project. Amie has been building marketing the same way at Dimagi, as a shared service that supports all six divisions.

Amie and Jonathan unpack the hard parts of standing up a horizontal capability: earning trust and buy-in, balancing big strategic bets against day-to-day requests, empowering a team to push back, and shifting the conversation from whether a request is useful to whether it is the most valuable use of time.

“Good shared services should have an infinite backlog. That's why they make sense to create a shared service in the first place. And shifting into a value-based discussion is the key unlock.”

Jonathan Jackson, on running a horizontal team

In this episode

  • Flipping the script: Jonathan interviews Amie on the career path from psychology and consulting to social-impact product marketing
  • Digital as a horizontal foundation: why projects fail when they stay siloed in one vertical and cannot add value across an organization
  • Earning the mandate: building buy-in and trust with no clear mandate, and why Jonathan made Amie earn it
  • Big rocks vs. blocking and tackling: balancing step-change strategic work against the constant flow of division requests
  • From reactive to proactive: shifting marketing from executing requests to partnering on strategy and understanding the real problem
  • Useful vs. valuable: why almost every ask is useful, and how a value-based discussion is the key to prioritizing a shared service
