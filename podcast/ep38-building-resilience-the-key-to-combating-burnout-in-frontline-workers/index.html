About this episode

In a world where stress is inevitable and burnout is rife, resilience is the acquired ability to recover, adapt, and grow from stress. In this episode, Jonathan Jackson sits down with Dimagi's Lilianna Bagnoli, Associate Director of Delivery for the India division and the team's informal mental health lead, to unpack how building resilience can guard frontline workers against burnout, defined by the World Health Organization as fatigue, detachment from work, and reduced professional efficacy.

Lilianna lays out the global picture, where about one in eight people live with a mental disorder yet specialist care is in short supply, and walks through the four interventions advocated by the 2018 Lancet Commission: task sharing, digital technologies, the balanced care model, and increasing demand. She then introduces WellMe, Dimagi's newly launched application built on CommCare that equips frontline workers with resilience-building behaviors through short courses, journal prompts, quizzes, and practice activities.

The conversation digs into hard lessons from user testing with drivers and delivery partners in Delhi, where the value proposition of mental health support collided with the daily pressure to earn enough to get by, and closes with a candid reflection on resilience at the organizational level, from incentives and fair pay to Dimagi's own efforts to take care of its staff.

“Addressing just the individual level, which is what we are doing with resilience, is important, but shouldn't be confused for being sufficient as a response to the stressors that frontline workers face.”

Lilianna Bagnoli

In this episode

  • Resilience as a skill: why it can be learned and practiced, and how it acts as a buffer against chronic workplace stress
  • Defining burnout: the WHO's three markers of fatigue, detachment, and reduced professional efficacy as a workplace phenomenon

  • Introducing WellMe: Dimagi's CommCare-based app delivering resilience courses, journal prompts, quizzes, and practice activities
