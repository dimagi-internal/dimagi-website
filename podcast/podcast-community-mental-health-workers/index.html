About this episode

Imagine a country of 50 million people with only 50 psychiatrists. That is the reality in Uganda, and Pavel Reppo is proving that specialists are not the only ones who can deliver life-changing care. As Executive Director of Finemind and CEO of the Matchbox Fund, he brings a rare dual perspective, bridging grassroots mental health work and the funding that sustains it.

We dig into how Finemind upskills primary health workers, midwives and nurses, to deliver evidence-based mental health support right at the point of care. Pavel makes the case that real impact is measured by functional recovery, children returning to school or a household with more reliable food security, not just a lower score on a clinical depression scale.

It is a hopeful, practical look at task-shifting done well, and a clear-eyed account of what it takes to close a treatment gap this wide.

In this episode

  • The staggering gap: 50 million people, roughly 50 psychiatrists
  • How Finemind upskills nurses and midwives to deliver care
  • Why functional recovery matters more than symptom scores alone
  • The Matchbox Fund and sustaining grassroots mental health work
  • What effective task-shifting can teach the rest of global health
