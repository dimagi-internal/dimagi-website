Flipping the Script on Aid: Five Talents’ Journey to Participatory Impact

What if communities, not donors, defined success, owned their data, and led the way? Five Talents on savings-led, community-owned development across Eastern Africa.

Listen on Spotify Apple Podcasts Amazon Music

About this episode

For decades, aid has flowed from the top down, with donors setting the goals and measuring the results. This episode asks what changes when you flip that script and put communities in charge of defining success and owning their own data.

Rachel Lindley, Hannah Wichmann, and Samantha Muli of Five Talents join the show to share an approach that goes well beyond finance. Through women-led savings groups across Eastern Africa, Five Talents builds trust, visibility, and inclusion at every level, with technology like CommCare strengthening the work rather than extracting from it.

The conversation digs into a specific program tackling human-wildlife conflict in Kenya's Tsavo Conservation Area, and steps back to ask what a more participatory, locally led future for aid could really look like.

In this episode

  • What it means to let communities define success and own their data
  • Savings groups as a platform for women's economic empowerment
  • The Tsavo program: addressing human-wildlife conflict locally
  • How CommCare strengthens trust, visibility, and inclusion
  • Lessons for funders on shifting power toward the communities they serve
