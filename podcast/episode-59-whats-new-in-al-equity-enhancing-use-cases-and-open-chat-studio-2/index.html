About this episode

In part three of Dimagi's series on artificial intelligence, Jonathan Jackson sits down with Brian DeRenzi, Dimagi's Research and Data team lead, to take stock of how fast the field has moved in the six months since their last conversation. They discuss the rapid pace of foundational model releases, why the improvements are shifting rather than slowing, and what all of this means for global health.

The conversation centers on how AI can be leveraged to improve health outcomes, support frontline health workers rather than replace them, and ensure equitable access to these tools. Brian and Jon walk through three areas of work, direct-to-client uses, coaching for health workers, and program manager assistants, alongside Dimagi's push into low-resource languages and open source models. They also dig into Open Chat Studio, Dimagi's platform for quickly building, testing, and deploying chatbots, and the harder strategic question of where AI fits inside CommCare, SureAdhere, and Dimagi's other products.

  • Why AI isn't really slowing down: four major model releases in a few months, and why the improvements are shifting rather than stalling
