The Thinking You Can’t Skip: AI for Good at a Crossroads

Part four of our AI for Good series. Dimagi's Brian DeRenzi on what AI still cannot do, hidden bias in frontier models, and the thinking you cannot outsource.

Listen on Spotify Apple Podcasts Amazon Music

About this episode

In part four of our AI for Good series, hosts Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro reconnect with Brian DeRenzi, Dimagi's VP of AI and Research, to explore how fast AI is evolving, and what it still cannot do. The technology has made staggering leaps, even as funding cuts have rocked the global health sector, creating real tension between potential and reality.

Brian shares why AI accelerates human intention but can never replace critical thinking. We dig into Dimagi's research on hidden bias in frontier models and on AI's performance in low-resource languages, the risks of what they call AI pilotitis, and why you still have to review transcripts by hand.

Jonathan's rule of thumb anchors the conversation: only build AI projects you are ready to leave on at scale. It is practical wisdom for anyone trying to apply AI responsibly right now.

Jonathan Jackson, Dimagi
