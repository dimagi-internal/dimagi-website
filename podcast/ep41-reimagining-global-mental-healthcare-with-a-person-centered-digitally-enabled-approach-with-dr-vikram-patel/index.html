About this episode

Dr. Vikram Patel is a psychiatrist, researcher, and co-founder of Sangath, an award-winning Indian NGO, and the incoming chair of Harvard Medical School's Department of Global Health and Social Medicine, where he succeeds the late Dr. Paul Farmer. He traces his path from training as a psychiatrist in London to working in Zimbabwe in the mid-1990s, where he found a country of roughly 9 million people served by only nine or ten psychiatrists and realized that almost everything he had learned was impossible to practice. That experience, set against the HIV pandemic in Southern Africa, began his journey into global mental health.

Across decades of field-defining research, Patel has shown that community-based resources and non-specialist frontline workers can meet a growing burden of mental illness. He explains the distinction between mental health and mental illness, why there is no health without mental health, and how his team distilled complex psychotherapy down to a single active ingredient, building a six-session depression treatment delivered by people with no prior mental health training. The conversation covers his push away from a rigid diagnostic model toward person-centered, staged care, and the measurement-based peer supervision approach developed with Dimagi to scale quality, not just training. Patel closes on why the future of technology in mental health is amplifying the human provider, not replacing them.

“The mental health app industry is a graveyard, a graveyard of beautiful ideas. Very strong commitment, a real mission, but sadly missing the most important ingredient, which is the provider.”

Dr. Vikram Patel
