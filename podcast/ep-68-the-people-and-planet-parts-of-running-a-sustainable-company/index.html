About this episode

In this episode, hosts Jonathan Jackson and Amie Vaccaro are joined by two leaders from Dimagi's People Operations team, Emily Gutman and Molly Broderick, to explore what it takes to run Dimagi in a way that is sustainable for both its employees and the planet. As a benefit corporation, Dimagi treats sustainability as a suitcase term spanning people, profit, and planet, and the conversation digs into the social and environmental sides of that balance.

On the people side, Molly describes what being employee first means in practice: putting the employee experience at the center of every decision across more than 20 countries, even when it adds significant logistical load. She walks through a two-year effort to scrap and rebuild Dimagi's performance management system around the company's values, while Emily traces the environmental journey from a 100 percent renewable office to climate neutral certification and annual carbon offsetting to reach net zero.

The group is candid about the hard parts, from the controversy and gray areas around carbon offset markets to the tension between in-person impact, business travel, and AI's growing energy footprint. They close with practical advice: measure early, reduce before you offset, lean on frameworks like B Corp and Climate Neutral, and use your business voice to push for climate policy.

“There's a way to treat people like people, which sometimes is actually harder to do and stay legally compliant. And what we always try to do is balance those things.”

Jonathan Jackson, on the Dimagi way
