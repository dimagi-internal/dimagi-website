About this episode

Dimagi recently published its five-year strategy for high-impact growth. In this episode, the first in a series on Strategic Priority #2, Sustain Exponential Growth, Jonathan Jackson is joined by Dimagi's Director of Engineering, Danny Roberts, to unpack a defining ethos: Dimagi prioritizes platforms over projects.

Amie Vaccaro frames why exponential here means exponential impact and not revenue. With CommCare already the largest frontline worker platform at just over 100,000 active users, but 10 million frontline workers still to reach, linear growth will not get there. The answer is setting partners up to scale on their own without needing Dimagi staff, and treating a frontline workforce as a delivery platform rather than a set of one-off, disease-specific projects.

Danny shares his 11-year journey from joining a small consultancy to helping build CommCare into a recognizable product, and explains how Dimagi's best features have come from individual projects that pushed the platform to its limits. The team accepts that most funding in global development is project based, then designs its approach to turn that funding into open-source global goods every partner can build on.

“All of our best features and kind of most innovative changes to CommCare over the last 10 years, have all come from working on individual projects and realizing that even if we stretched the existing functionality of the platform to its limit, it just wasn't going to be able to do it.”

Danny Roberts

In this episode

  • Impact, not revenue: why exponential growth for Dimagi means reaching far more frontline workers, not growing the top line
  • The platform analogy: organizations buy paper rather than run their own paper mill, and CommCare aims to be that always-available blank canvas
  • Setting partners free: designing projects so they can scale from 1,000 to 10,000 users without Dimagi staff or extra headcount
  • Custom versus product: the recurring trade-off between building one project's feature and investing 10x to make it useful for everyone
  • From custom reports to a mature ecosystem: how data extraction and integration tools ended the cycle of writing one-off reports
  • Open source as strategy: accepting that most funding in the field is project based, then turning it into global goods every partner benefits from
  • Platforms for health systems: treating the frontline workforce as a delivery platform across HIV, TB, and maternal and child health, not siloed projects
