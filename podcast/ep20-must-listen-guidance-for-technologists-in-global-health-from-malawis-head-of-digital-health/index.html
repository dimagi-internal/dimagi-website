Jonathan Jackson talks to Simeon Yosefe, Head of Digital Health for the Ministry of Health of Malawi, to discuss the challenges and opportunities of digitally transforming Malawi’s health system. You’ll hear about the importance of aligning digital health systems closely to the vision and digital health strategy of the government, how Malawi is creating policy guidance to ensure that its digital health systems work for Malawi, and why we need to take a comprehensive approach to digital systems. Malawi has been a leader in digital health laying out a bold digital health strategy and this episode is a must listen for any digital health practitioners working or wanting to work with Ministries of Health.

In this episode

  • From statistician to Head of Digital Health: Simeon Yosefe's path into transforming how Malawi manages health data
  • 40 systems and counting: an assessment that surfaced duplicated, siloed, partner-named tools, many already abandoned
  • Harmonizing work plans: pooling partner priorities to see where resources cluster and where critical needs go neglected
  • Infrastructure over dongles: pushing partners away from short-term fixes toward long-term connectivity investments
  • Policy with teeth: drafting an enforcement framework so alignment survives when grant deliverables conflict
  • Beyond the vertical program: caring for the whole patient who may have HIV, TB, and a pregnancy at once
  • Shared value: how to reevaluate whether a multi-year digital investment is delivering the health outcome it promised
